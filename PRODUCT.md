# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People converting Swedish coordinate data in a browser: surveyors, developers, GIS-adjacent users, and anyone pasting CSVs or uploading files to get coordinates into the right projection quickly. They are in a task flow, usually with source data in front of them and a need for fast, trustworthy output.

## Product Purpose

Convert coordinate data between SWEREF99, RT90, and WGS84 with as little friction as possible. Success means the user can inspect, convert, verify on the map, and export the result without confusion or unnecessary interface weight.

## Brand Personality

Fast, direct, dependable.

The interface should feel like a practical instrument panel: clear, compact, and confident, with no decorative flourishes that slow the task down.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS polish, glossy gradients, over-rounded cards, playful dashboard styling, and anything that feels like a marketing site pretending to be a tool. Avoid surprising interactions, heavy ornament, and visual noise that competes with the data.

## Design Principles

- Prioritize speed of comprehension over visual novelty.
- Let the conversion workflow stay obvious from input to output.
- Keep the component vocabulary consistent across forms, tables, dialogs, and map view.
- Use contrast and spacing to guide attention, not decoration.
- Make dense data readable without making the surface feel cramped.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, keyboard accessibility, reduced-motion support, and readable tables/forms at small and large viewport sizes. Treat the map as a verification aid, not the only way to understand the result.
